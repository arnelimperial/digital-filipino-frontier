Tagaytay Highlands

Development expanded throughout the early 2000s. A premier residential resort community that reflects lifestyle changes among affluent Filipinos.

Intramuros Rehabilitation (2000-2010)

Efforts to rehabilitate and restore Intramuros, the historic walled city of Manila, during this period aimed at promoting heritage tourism and cultural education, emphasizing the richness of Philippine history.

The Subic-Clark-Tarlac Expressway (SCTEX)

a 93.77-kilometer expressway that connects the provinces of Bataan, Pampanga, and Tarlac, improving transportation and economic development in the region.

The Mind Museum (2012, Planning began in 2005)

Though opened in 2012, the planning and development of The Mind Museum started in the late 2000s, aiming to promote scientific literacy through interactive exhibits, thus fostering a culture of learning in a modern context

University of the Philippines Diliman Campus Redevelopment (2008)

The redevelopment of UP Diliman included the enhancement of green spaces within the university, emphasizing ecological sensitivity while fostering a vibrant academic community

Vigan Heritage Village

Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1999 but gained popularity throughout the early 2000s. The preservation efforts highlighted local culture and tourism significance.

Cultural Center of the Philippines (CCP) Complex Improvements (2000-2010)

Improvements to the CCP Complex aimed at enhancing its facilities for performances and exhibitions, solidifying its role as a hub for arts and culture in the Philippines and attracting local and international events.

SM Mall of Asia

Opened in 2006, this mall became a cultural hub, hosting events and gatherings, influencing shopping and leisure culture in Metro Manila.

Bonifacio Global City (BGC)

This area underwent significant development during this period, transforming from a military base to a bustling urban center known for its modern office buildings, residential spaces, and lifestyle hubs.

Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park

Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1999, it gained international recognition during this decade for its unique biodiversity and geological features, attracting eco-tourism.
